Amazon Coding Test Format:

Example Coding Questions:
*   **Question 1 (Strings)**: Check if strings are rotations of each other.
    *   Input: Two strings s1 and s2.
    *   Task: Determine if s2 is a rotated version of s1.
    *   Expected Time Complexity: O(N)
    *   Expected Space Complexity: O(N)
*   **Question 2 (Strings/Palindrome)**: Jarvis is weak in computing palindromes for Alphanumeric characters.
    *   Input: A string containing alphanumeric characters.
    *   Task: Find out whether the string is a palindrome, ignoring symbols, whitespaces, and case.
    *   Expected Time Complexity: O(N)
    *   Expected Space Complexity: O(1)
*   **Question 3 (Binary Tree)**: Given a binary tree, print the bottom view from left to right.
    *   Task: Return an array containing the bottom view of the given tree. If multiple nodes are at the same horizontal distance and bottom-most, print the later one in level traversal.
    *   Expected Time Complexity: O(N)
    *   Expected Auxiliary Space: O(N)
*   **Question 4 (Stack/Expression Evaluation)**: Evaluate a postfix expression.
    *   Input: A postfix expression with basic arithmetic operators ('*', '/', '+', '-'), multiple-digit operands, and space separators. No brackets.
    *   Task: Evaluate the expression, outputting the answer modulo (10^9+7). Use modular division when required.

Overall Summary & Key Takeaways (Deloitte Analyst Perspective):

The Amazon SDE interview process is rigorous, heavily weighted towards technical proficiency, particularly in Data Structures and Algorithms, but also places significant emphasis on behavioral aspects and cultural fit through its Leadership Principles.

Common Themes Across Sites:
*   **DSA is Paramount**: All sources stress the critical importance of Data Structures and Algorithms. This is the core of technical rounds and the online assessment.
*   **Behavioral & Leadership Principles**: Amazon's 16 Leadership Principles are a non-negotiable part of the interview, especially in the Bar Raiser and HR rounds. The STAR method is recommended for answering behavioral questions.
*   **Structured Interview Process**: A multi-stage process involving an online assessment, multiple technical rounds, an HR round, and a Bar Raiser round.
*   **System Design**: Confirmed as a dedicated round by InterviewBit, though GeeksforGeeks mentions it in course titles, implying its relevance.
*   **Project Discussion**: Projects are a key part of technical rounds, requiring deep understanding and confidence in your work.
*   **Computer Science Fundamentals**: OOPs, DBMS, Operating Systems are tested via MCQs and conceptual questions.

**Top 5 DSA Topics (Aggregated from all sources):**
1.  **Arrays**: Fundamental, with problems ranging from basic manipulation to complex subarray sums and matrix traversals.
2.  **Strings**: Common for pattern matching, manipulation, and palindrome checks.
3.  **Linked Lists**: Essential for understanding dynamic memory and pointer manipulation.
4.  **Trees (including Binary Trees and BSTs)**: Crucial for advanced data organization, traversal, and recursive problem-solving. Graph problems often build on tree concepts.
5.  **Dynamic Programming**: A frequent topic for hard-level problems, requiring optimization over recursion. Greedy Algorithms are also prominent for medium-level problems.

**Must-Know Leadership Principles (Implicitly and Explicitly from InterviewBit):**
*   **Customer Obsession**: Always start with the customer and work backward.
*   **Ownership**: Take responsibility for results.
*   **Invent and Simplify**: Innovate and find simpler solutions.
*   **Are Right, A Lot**: Seek diverse perspectives and challenge decisions.
*   **Learn and Be Curious**: Continuously seek new knowledge and explore possibilities.
*   (Candidates should be prepared to discuss real-life examples for these and other LPs using the STAR method.)

Key Behavioral Interview Advice:
*   **STAR Method**: Essential for structuring answers to behavioral questions (Situation, Task, Action, Result).
*   **Show Enthusiasm and Curiosity**: Ask thoughtful questions about the company, technology, and team.
*   **Highlight Strengths**: Confidently articulate your skills and achievements.
*   **Cultural Fit**: Demonstrate alignment with Amazon's Leadership Principles.

System Design Emphasis:
*   While not extensively detailed with specific problems, its inclusion as a dedicated round signifies its importance for SDE roles. Candidates should prepare for designing scalable, reliable, and efficient systems.

In summary, aspiring Amazon SDEs must master DSA, practice extensively, understand and exemplify the Leadership Principles, and be ready to discuss their projects in depth. Preparation should cover a wide range of DSA topics, with a focus on Arrays, Strings, Linked Lists, Trees, and Dynamic Programming, alongside core CS fundamentals and system design concepts.
